Vehicles FAQ

Your vehicle platform determines which mods fit, how much power you can safely gain, and how dyno results should be interpreted. This page explains the “why” before you pick bolt-ons or run performance-per-dollar (PPD) numbers.

Why platform matters

  • Fitment: intakes, downpipes, and intercoolers are platform-specific.
  • Baseline power: a +40hp gain feels different on a 180hp car vs. a 350hp car.
  • Limits: fueling, cooling, and transmission/clutch capacity set real boundaries.
  • Consistency: dyno numbers vary by dyno type, conditions, and gear used.

Common examples

These are just quick examples of how different vehicle types change mod strategy.

  • Turbo 4-cyl (MQB / WRX / CTR): intake + downpipe + tune often gives big gains.
  • NA sports cars (BRZ/86): bolt-ons help, but gains are smaller without forced induction.
  • V8 platforms (Mustang/Camaro): traction, cooling, and supporting mods matter a lot.
  • AWD performance (GTR / Golf R): drivetrain handles power well, but heat management is key.
